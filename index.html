Acca sellowiana

Native to South America, the flowers shown here grow on Acca , shrubs and small trees which are cultivated for their edible fruits known as feijoas or pineapple guavas.

Abelmoschus

These capsule-like fruits come from the Abelmoschus , a genus of flowering plants formerly included within the Hibiscus classification. They can be found in tropical Africa, Asia, and northern Australia.

Aechmea

The botanical Aechmea 's name comes from "aichme", the Greek word for spear. These plants are native to Mexico and South America.

Acer

More commonly referred to as maple, Acer is a genus of trees and shrubs that are distributed all over the world and consist of approximately 128 different species.

Adromischus

This flower was found blooming on the Adromischus , a leaf succulent. Originating in southern Africa, the name comes from the Greek words "adros" and "mischos", meaning "thick" and "stem".

Annona

A member of the pawpaw or sugar apple family, Annona are known for producing edible and nutritious fruits. They are also used to treat a variety of diseases, though their medicinal purposes have not yet been scientifically validated.
